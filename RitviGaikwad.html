All About ISRO

By Ritvi Gaikwad

Just among other internship experiences you guys might have read, this one is about the most reputed organization in India. An organization with beautiful brains filled with galaxies, planets, and stars. For us, those are just stars but for them, it’s a new chance to explore more about our space. This specific organization where I got in is situated in Ahmedabad, Gujarat and this is how I got in.

How, When and Why
At first, I was trying to get an internship through college as usual. But simultaneously I was applying for ISRO off-campus student program. I was not sure that I will get an internship in ISRO, so I never fully dropped the idea of not applying for other companies through college. Then I filled out the student program form for ISRO and applied with my friend Rutuja Deshpande in the month of March. With no response for half a month, we lost hope. During the process of Suez waters on campus, we got a response from ISRO. We received a mail that our applications have been approved and our interviews have been scheduled.

We got excited but had no idea what to prepare. We researched the questionnaire online and found whatever was related. Finally, the day arrived and these were the questions they asked:

  • Which projects have you completed?
  • On which software have you worked?
  • Why should we have you?
  • What qualities in you can bring a change in our organization?
  • Which domains do you want to work for? And why?
  • And many related to the projects and core basics.

Again, the wait was long, and we got a response finally in the mid of May and got our acceptance letter. In which they mentioned our mentor and the department we will be working. We got into Antenna mechanical design division (AMDD). The training program took place for a week and then we were assigned our projects.

Getting a chance to explore a National organization like ISRO is like a dream come true for every individual who aspires to join it since their childhood. Just like a small kid in a fair, admiring giant wheels and trying to capture everything which amuses her in those small eyes which are never going to be enough, I was astonished by the amazing work and the projects taking place in ISRO. It was a complete honor to get a chance to experience some unbelievable miracles over there so closely.

Talking about my project, it was a research-based project. During the project, I was working on Ansys and NX software platforms. My knowledge of core subjects like Strength of materials, Machine Design, Materials technology, and analysis and synthesis of mechanisms was very important and turned out to be very useful. Buckling, bending moment, and material properties study were some key concepts.

For those who are aspiring to work for ISRO, it’s a great learning exposure for every individual. The internship has truly been a wonderful experience for me. The knowledge and experience which you will gain during the internship is definitely going to be beneficial to you in future projects and roles. It will not only strengthen your resume but also boost your confidence and develop you individually as well as professionally.

I want to express my deep gratitude to ISRO (Space applications center) for providing me with such a great opportunity.
